20. Change into negative and interrogative form 5 sentences bellow! 1.Erik and Samuel visit their mother at siloam hospital 2.She bought the dictionary last morning 3.Your mother was here last night 4.Zara is beautiful today 5.They are so kind


Bentuk negatif dan interrogatif dari kalimat di atas adalah..

1). (-) Erik and Samuel do not visit their mother at Siloam hospital.

(?) Do Erik and Samuel visit their mother at Siloam hospital?

2). (-) She did not buy the dictionary last morning.

(?) Did she buy the dictionary last morning?

3). (-) Your mother was not here last night.

(?) Was your mother here last night?

4). (-) Zara is not beautiful today.

(?) Is Zara beautiful today?

5). (-) They are not so kind.

(?) Are they so kind?

PEMBAHASAN

Sebelum kita mengubah kalimat diatas ke dalam bentuk negatif (?) atau tanya (?), maka kita harus pastikan dulu jenis tenses yang digunakan dalam kalimatnya. Dan pada kalimat diatas ada 2 jenis tenses yang digunakan yaitu :

Simple Present (Verbal & Nominal) Simple Past (Verbal & Nominal)

Simple Present

↪ Tenses yang menjelaskan tentang suatu kebenaran umum dan kebiasaan sehari-hari yang dilakukan pada masa sekarang.

Rumus Simple Present Verbal

(+) S + Verb-1 (s/es/ies) + O

Erik and Samuel visit their mother at Siloam hospital.

(-) S + Do/does + not + Verb-1 + O

Erik and Samuel do not visit their mother at Siloam hospital.

(?) Do/does + S + Verb-1 + O

Do Erik and Samuel visit their mother at Siloam hospital?

Rumus Simple Present Nominal

(+) S + To be (am/are/is) + complement

Zara is beautiful today. They are so kind.

(-) S + To be (am/are/is) + not + complement

Zara is not beautiful today. They are not so kind.

(?) To be (am/are/is) + S + complement

Is Zara beautiful today? Are they so kind?

Simple Past

↪ Tenses yang menjelaskan tentang kegiatan yang terjadi pada masa lampau.

Rumus Simple Past Verbal

(+) S + Verb-2 + O

She bought the dictionary last morning.

(-) S + Did + not + Verb-1 + O

She did not buy the dictionary last morning.

(?) Did + S + Verb-1 + O

Did she buy the dictionary last morning?

Rumus Simple Past Nominal

(+) S + To be (was/were) + complement

Your mother was here last night.

(-) S + To be (was/were) + not + complement

Your mother was not here last night.

(?) To be (was/were) + S + complement

Was your mother here last night?
